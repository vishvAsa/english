Last will & testament

Nikhil Surya Dwibhashyam, 13 Nov. 2023 ed. 7 Jan. 2026

This document constitutes my last will and testament, superseding all physical or electronic documents whose date præcedes this document’s date of last significant emendation shown above. If I be married at the time of my death, my Wife is permitted to override any stipulation in this document as she sees fit; none else is permitted to override even a single stipulation in this document. A wife is here defined as a woman whose marriage to me has been established by a Vēdic wedding (vivāhá) in which a bull or a cow is slain (gav-ālambhá), seven steps are taken by the fire (sapta-padī́), and the right hand is seized (hasta-grāhá). Thus, a woman with whom I am merely in a long-term relationship, or even a woman to whom I am legally considered married by some government’s system of “common law” without having completed a proper Vēdic wedding, cannot constitute a wife for the purposes of this document. (Conversely, a woman who has been Vēdically but not legally wed to me does constitute a wife.)

If I be married and survived by such a wife, she is to be the executor of this will; otherwise my eldest living Vēdist male son who has attained the age of legal majority; otherwise my eldest living Vēdist daughter who has attained the age of legal majority; otherwise I charge my close friends collectively as executors. A Vēdist is a self-declared follower of revivalist Vēdic scriptural fundamentalism, who rejects Hinduism. A close friend (Busenfreund, aryamáṇ) is here defined as a male Ā́rya or Şūdrá with whom I have sworn an oath of mutual loyalty containing «sakhyā́t tē mā́ yōṣam». The duty of an executor is to have all stipulations in this will executed without exception by any legal or illegal means necessary, even if a court-system seek to override a stipulation. (For example, if a court violate my will by ordering my body præserved, an executor ought to try to arrange for my body to be stolen.)

Brain death

In the event of brain-death, in which my conscious mind is irrecoverably lost and my soul has therefore fled my body, my body should not be kept on life-support for any length of time longer than necessary to confirm the brain-death, and my will should be executed. In the event of brain-death not requiring life-support, my body should be allowed to waste away to death through starvation, and my will should be executed (even before my body dies). In the event of an injury irrecoverably præventing me from at all communicating but not causing brain-death, I should be kept alive and healthy but all portions of my will not related to my corpse and funeral should be executed.

Missing

In the event that I go missing for more than six months, I should be præsumed dead.

Organs and blood

My organs and blood are not to be donated in any capacity nor frozen nor embalmed. They are not to be extracted from my body for any reason other than an autopsy, which may only be performed if there be legitimate and important intrigue surrounding the circumstances of my death (e.g. a suspicion that I be the victim of murder); such an autopsy is to be so noninvasive and urgent as possible, with all organs and blood placed back into their correct positions after analysis. No part of my body, not even a single hair, may be præserved: My entire body is to be cremated as one on a large open-air pyre of wood. If my body be missing or otherwise truly unavailable, a substitute corpse is to be fashioned out of three hundred sixty leaf-stalks and burned. If my body then be found after my funeral, it should still be burned completely so soon as possible.

Rites

My funeral rites are to be performed by well-born Brāhmaṇá priests following the Vēdic rites so much as possible: ŞB 12.5.2 if I be married and have set up the sacred fires, or else a rite that follows a GS and in which the hymns R̥V 10.14–18 are recited at the cremation. I plan to provide much more specific detail on the instructions for both these forms of the funeral in future versions of this will; if I die before then, my funeral should be conducted based on careful research by members of Rikthá.

Atendence

Any Vēdist may attend my funeral. All Hindus (those adhering to Postvēdic Indian religion) are to be strictly barred from my funeral, with two categories of exceptions:

  1. Ā́rya descendants of any of my great-grandfathers and those descendants’ Ā́rya husbands or Ā́ryā or Şūdrā́ wives, and
  2. the priests to perform my rites, only if insufficient Vēdists be found to perform them.

All Şūdrá‑s are to be strictly barred from my funeral except executors and those mentioned incidentally somewhere in this will.

Marker

I would like a thin rectangular grave-stone made of granite (or else another igneous rock) approximately three feet in height, engraved so close as possible to the following (the font unimportant, præferably serifless), with great care taken to avoid orthographic mistakes.

Smárata Níkhilasūryam Dvibhāṣyàm
yás máryas Pítryadharmas Dēvayús
Svargḗ jyṓk rámātāi.⊙

«Nítyē táva‿asmy amútra ca.»

The grave-stone should be located next to a tree near a clear, cold stream on a snowy, unpopulated mountain. The mountain should præferably be among the Himālayá‑s, but if I be banned from the Republic of India, the grave-stone should be located in a mountain range in the United States of America.

Buried approximately six feet beneath my grave-stone I would like a wooden (not stone) box with thin walls containing my ashes in an earthen urn, my svastiká pendant, seven uncooked grains of rice, seven small (approximately one-gram) disks of fairly pure gold, and seven stalks of the sṓma-plant, Ephedra distachya. The location of the grave-site should not be disclosed to nonexecutors.

Inheritence

The only descendants of mine eligible to inherit my property are Vēdists; for example, if I specify “my eldest male son” in this document, I refer to “my eldest Vēdist male son”. My property is to be divided among my living male sons (including fētūs) equally, except that the eldest is to receive double the normal share. If have no living sons but have living grandsons through deceased sons, they should be treated like sons, and likewise in the case of great-grandsons if all my grandsons be deceased.

So long as I have at least one living patrilineal male descendant, none of my female family is entitled to inherit my property; but if I do not, then all my property is to go to my wife, and only if I have exclusively female descendants but no living wife should my property be divided among them as specified above for my male descendants (among daughters, otherwise among granddaughters, &c.). In all cases, none of my property is to end up in the possession of a family-member not married to or descended from me.

If I die without living wife nor living Vēdist descendants, my property is to be divided as follows. My pistol, my ammunition, my knives, and all my other weapons-related possessions are to go to QHJ, as are my truck and all my other vehicle-related possessions.

All my pens and inks are to go to DhG.

All my books and written notes related to the Vēdic religion and language are to go to KSA.

All my other books and written notes are to go to KOLR.

All my other possessions, including pets and live-stock, as well as all my money accessible to the executors of this will, whether in cash, gold, cryptocurrency, or bank accounts, are to be divided approximately æqually among Vēdist members of Rikthá.

If following one of the individual-specific instructions be impossible (e.g. because of the death of the intended recipient), the property in question should likewise be divided among Rikthá.

E-data

If I have no living patrilineal male descendant, all web-sites that I run are to be transferred (vis-à-vis domain and VPS) to KSA. In all cases, my personal web-site is to be edited with a new post announcing my death, fully following my orthographic rules, and corrections of spelling errors and citation-errors are to be made continuously as they are noticed. The PHP code restricting access to my personal web-site by certain IP addresses may be removed; otherwise no modifications should be made to my personal web-site. My other web-sites should be operated as normal. My flash-drives, hard drives, and other storage-media are not to be decrypted in any way nor backed up, and are to be wiped securely several times (i.e. overwritten with random data) before being thoroughly destroyed chemically and physically and discarded in an obscure dump. The same applies to any existing backups or other copies of my storage-media.

Debts

Any debts that I owe to individuals are to be paid in full through any cash that I have saved, with any remaining debt paid off through the sale of as many of my possessions as necessary, superseding instructions in the prævious paragraphs. If it be legally possible to avoid paying any debts that I owe to private corporate entities, it should be avoided; otherwise, they are to be paid in the same way as my debts to individuals. Any inheritance-tax is to be avoided so much as possible by legal or illegal means. In the event of an imminent seizure of any of my property by a government, the property is to be destroyed rather than allowed to fall into the government’s hands: For example, my live-stock should be sacrificed en masse rather than surrendered. And none of my property whatsoever, monetary or not, is ever to be given to a charity.
