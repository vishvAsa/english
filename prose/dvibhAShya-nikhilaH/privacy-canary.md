### Privacy policy

My web-site is to be accessed only by readers aged twelve years or older. I do not believe that younger children should be allowed on the Internet by their parents. Note also that several pages on my web-site deal with sexual themes, from a religious stand-point.

  

My web-site uses (first-party, HTTPOnly) cookies for its pass-word protection and to prævent access by mobile or hostile users, and runs a small JS script mainly to help prævent access by mobile users. No other cookies are created or read and no other tracking is employed. As the (sole) administrator of this web-site, I have access to the following information about you based on your HTTP request. I do not collect any other information about users.

- Your Internet Protocol (IP) address, `...`
    
    - From your IP address: your location (with typically city-level præcision)
    
    - From your IP address: your Internet service provider

- Your [user-agent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/User-Agent_header)
    
    - From your user-agent (usually): which browser you are using, and which version
    
    - From your user-agent (usually): your operating system
    
    - However, it is possible and even common to spoof the user-agent. User-agent spoofing is also how mobile browsers request a site’s desk-top version.

- Which pages on this web-site you accessed, and when
    - I cannot tell how long you spent reading each page, except by æstimation based on data like the span of time before you access another page on this web-site.
- (Approximately) which link you clicked to access a page on this web-site: the [HTTP Referer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HTTP_referer) \[sīc\]
    
    - The default for most browsers is to send me the full URL if you access a page on this web-site through a link on this web-site, but only the domain name if you access a page on this web-site through a link elsewhere.
    
    - It is possible to disable sending the Referer on your browser, but this causes many web-sites not to work.

There should be no expectation of a right to privacy in using my web-site. I reserve the right to read and to publish information (aggregate or not) about my readers at will; in fact, if I can very confidently identify IP addresses belonging to certain individuals who have published my private information or violated my copy-right, I have no compunction about [publishing](../stalkers/) those IP addresses for fun.

  

In practice, however, I do not publish any other information about my readers. There is no third-party advertising on my web-site (nor in any of my other work), and there never will be—I would rather shut the site down. No images, scripts, web-fonts, or other resources are loaded from a third party. A couple third-party APIs are queried securely with your IP address on first encounter for geolocation and proxy detection, and your IP address is also technically visible to the hosting service used by this web-site; aside from this, no information about readers is shared with third parties.

  

I reserve the right to ban any person or group of people at will from reading my web-site, including entire nations: California and the People’s Republic of China have been banned for most of the period since Aug. 2024, and Bihār was formerly banned. I regularly look through logs for suspicious activity, and have manually banned IP addresses in the past.

  

I reserve the right to amend this privacy policy at will, without notice.

### Canary

Beginning with the first full Moon after the winter Solstice of 2023, I have provided a monthly [canary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Warrant_canary) stating that I am alive and well and that I have not been contacted by a government about my web-site. If and only if I fail to provide an update by ten days after the last full Moon, suspicion should be aroused. If I fail to provide five updates in a row, I should be præsumed dead and my [last will and testament](../will/) should be executed.

  

The reason for this canary is twofold. First, I love solitude and have the habit of disappearing for long periods of time without contacting any friends; my record was thirty-five days in 2019. My updating this canary is proof that I have not actually gone missing, and that you need not worry about me.

  

Second, my web-site is not merely personal, and generates interest primarily for its religious–political content. This makes it potentially interesting to governments founded upon principles fundamentally opposed to my religion (—really every government currently in existence, but especially the Republic of India). My web-site is as yet of fairly little note, but if it were ever to become more well-known, it could easily be subject to bans by some of these governments. This canary serves as a notification primarily about attempts at such bans. It does not serve as a notification primarily about search-warrants; this web-site is hosted on a VPS located in the United States of America, and not on a personal computer, so I could not know about nor physically prævent a seizure of this server if the American security-state desired it.

  

No content on my web-site is illegal under any fœderal or state laws of the United States of America (though I myself am [likely](../past/#sra) on an undisclosed watch-list); nor is it illegal under those of Canada (my other nation of citizenship), where it is theoretically protected by §319.3b of the Criminal Code. However, much of the content on my web-site violates laws in several other countries; in the Republic of India, for example, where many of my readers live, this web-site is clearly illegal, violating at least §196.1a–b §196.2 §197.1a–c §197.2 §299 §353.1c §353.2–3 of the Bhāratī́ya Nyāya-samhitā́ (formerly §153A §153B §295A §505.1c §505.2–3 of the Indian Pœnal Code) and §3.1u–v of the Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes (Prævention of Atrocities) Act. Secretly I hope that this web-site be banned in India some day; it would be a badge of honor.
